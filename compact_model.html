The compact structural companion (compact11) INTERPRETATION-ONLY

An 11-variable structural BVAR whose job is exhaustive, quotable shock attribution — the interpretation layer beside the 32-variable production forecasting model, on the Bank of England MTP-3 template. It never produces the forecast.
What this model is. Eleven quarterly FRED series (1985Q1–), nine named structural shocks — global demand, energy supply, technology, labour supply, wage bargaining, US demand, monetary policy, financial conditions, uncertainty (financial/policy) — plus two structured residual disturbances, identified by impact-only exact zeros, sign restrictions and magnitude cross-rankings, drawn with the Arias-Rubio-Ramirez-Waggoner (2018) algorithm including its Algorithm-3 volume-weight correction (estimand: NGN-conditional, stamped on every artifact; on the launch-scheme GDP headline the weighted and unweighted medians differ by 0.5pp). Estimated homoskedastic with Cascaldi-Garcia pandemic dummies and a GLP-estimated single-unit-root prior — whose tightness the marginal likelihood set LOOSE, and the pre-registered stability ladder confirmed the ML choice beats tighter settings on the Bergholt roller-coaster metric.

For the nominal/policy block the posterior barely improves on the scheme-implied prior — CPI-U sits slightly BELOW its prior (84 vs 86) and Fed Funds only just above it (89 vs 86, bands nearly coincident): the restrictions, not the data, carry those two coverage numbers. That is one mechanism with two exhibits: the same two variables’ residuals are partially predictable from the 32-variable model’s information set (Forni-Gambetti p = 0.027 / 0.018) — the designed information gap of an 11-variable system with no term-structure variable. A 12-variable GS10 hybrid is a pre-registered robustness arm with a fixed materiality rule.

The distribution of structural shocks, quarter by quarter

The Bank-of-England-style diagnostic: posterior densities of each named shock at the latest quarter, the prior quarter and four quarters back, pooling parameter and identification uncertainty.
Within the identified set the allocation of residuals to shocks reflects the identification prior as well as the data (Baumeister-Hamilton 2015). “Technology” is the compact-model definition: it correlates with none of the production model’s twelve shocks (max |corr| 0.15) — the well-documented cross-method instability of technology-shock identification — and its series shows foresight contamination (Granger-predictable from SPF forecasts, p = 0.014), as do Global Demand (p = 0.0015) and, at the margin, Financial (p = 0.038). Monetary passes the Ramey battery (p = 0.199).

Method & governance

Scheme frozen at G1 and adopted at G3 by an adversarial red-team review (design record: docs/research/compact11_design_2026-07.md) with pre-registered gate criteria: the wage-bargaining split passed its triad-stability and IRF-distinctness bars (WB×Energy |corr| 0.31 ≤ 0.5); the uncertainty split adopted the order-invariant magnitude-ranking encoding over the spread-zero arm (shock correlation 0.26 vs 0.69 — the zero arm’s columns were substantially the same object). Identification diagnostics for this scheme: acceptance 9.9e-05 per rotation, ESS ratio 0.49, max weight 0.8%. Historical decompositions use the rolling 20-quarter window (companion max-root median 1.006 under the mechanical flavour rule). The model is re-estimated each vintage beside the production run; it never enters the forecast comparison.
