Design. Expanding-window pseudo-out-of-sample backtest at the production specification: 25 origins, 2012Q1–2024Q1 every 2 quarters; the model is fully re-estimated at each origin (order-invariant SV engine, 1,500 draws / 750 burn) and scored against realised outcomes at h = 1, 4, 8 quarters. Scores are computed in model space (log100 levels; rates ×100). Benchmarks: random walk and AR(1) fitted per variable on the same windows. Honesty caveats: outcomes are final-vintage, not first-release (a real-time ALFRED track is roadmap item R1); 25 origins give formal tests limited power; h=8 windows overlap heavily.

Reading guide

CRPS (continuous ranked probability score) scores the whole forecast density against the outcome — lower is better; it generalises absolute error and rewards both accuracy and honest uncertainty. The known weak cells are disclosed rather than hidden: long rates lose to the random walk at short horizons (a documented consequence of the levels design; the long-run anchors are the mitigation), and coverage sits slightly below nominal in some tails. Every number on this page comes from a committed, re-runnable evaluation harness; the race verdicts reference internal evidence packs, available on request.
